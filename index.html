Elias Lancaster

Elias Lancaster was an English biologist in the 18th and 19th centuries, infamous for his rumored studies in alchemy and disappearance in his later life.

Early Life

Lancaster was born in England in 1784, and moved throughout Europe in his lifetime. He was raised in a small English town before moving to London in 1798 at 14 years old. Reports from staff of Lancaster's primary school all state that other children saw him as "sometimes appearing monstrous."

Career

Lancaster attended a university in [REDACTED], where he started pursuing a career in medicine. However, in his second year in 1804, he began skipping classes and denying visitors to his apartment, before being presumed missing after a month with no contact from him. When his apartment was searched, many sketches and letters from him and his psychologist were found, but Lancaster was never discovered.

Mental Health

Lancaster was known to have mental health struggles, especially with what may have been psychotic symptoms. Lancaster's psychologist, Dr. Ervin French, was the only one that Lancaster kept in contact with during his disappearance, as inferred by their letters.

Modern Day

While improbable, there have been many sightings of men throughout Europe and North America that match the description and portrait sketches of Elias Lancaster.
