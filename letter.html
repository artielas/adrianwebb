Letter to Dr. Erwin French from Elias Lancaster - May 8th, 1804

Dear Dr. French,

I'm afraid I may become unable to come directly to your office for the foreseeable future; however the condition I discussed with you is improving. Do not worry for me, for the next time I see you, should it come, I hope to be rid of it.

If or when I should withdraw contact from my fellow-creatures, I ask that you conceal all artefacts you own that prove you know of this condition. You may send them to me if you truly do not want them destroyed; otherwise, I ask that if you can't hide them, then burn them. When I discover a way to cure myself of it, I wish that none speak of or suffer from it from then on.

Sincerely,

Elias Lancaster
